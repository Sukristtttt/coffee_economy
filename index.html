🤸 Production of Coffee

This map chart shows all countries with or without coffee bean production Worldwide.

  • Brazil produces the largest amount of coffee beans each year, with 3700231 tonnes.

  • Most countries do not produce coffee beans, with climate and geographical location being the main reasons.

  • So from the graph we can also infer again that the tropical climate around the equator is a suitable geographic location for coffee beans to grow. Therefore these countries, especially Brazil, produce a particularly large amount of coffee beans.

🧐 How about the Production Market?

  • There are over 100 coffee varieties, but the main commercial varieties are Arabica and Robusta.

  • Of these, Arabica dominates the production market, with a market share of about 60%.

  • From the stack chart,you can explore that the production of coffee beans is increasing due to the growing demand, which will be seen in the consumption segment.

    💰 Consumption of Coffee

    You can see the change difference by clicking on the toolbox or pulling.

      DEEMAND

    • we can see that coffee demand is rising by the number of kilograms of coffee consumed per capita.

    • However, in 2020, the overall coffee consumption decreases, due to the lack of labor and home isolation under Covid-19 that cannot be consumed, but gradually picks up in 2021.

      PRICE

    • Overall prices are going up, which is inevitable, but not in a regular way.

    • We can see that prices were down from 2000-2002 and peaked from then until May 2011, when they exceeded three dollars a pound.

    • After that it fell to the level of one dollar to one point five dollars repeatedly fluctuating until the arrival of COVID-19, due to the impact of demand, oversupply and subsequent inflation caused by the price of coffee again ushered in an upward trend.
